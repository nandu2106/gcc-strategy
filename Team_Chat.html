Use Cases & Personas

Who uses Teams, how they use it, and what they need

Persona-Driven Design: Teams must work for corporate office workers AND manufacturing plant operators AND executives AND external partners. Each persona has distinct needs, access patterns, and collaboration styles. I design for all of them.

User Personas & Collaboration Patterns

Corporate Knowledge Workers (6,000 users)

Who: Office staff, managers, project leads, cross-functional teams

Location: Corporate HQ, regional offices

Devices: Desktop + Mobile + Tablet

Primary Use Cases:
Daily 1:1 chats Project channels Daily standups Knowledge sharing

Communication Pattern: Heavy chat users (10-15 chats/day). Channel members (8-12 teams). 2-3 meetings daily. Files stored in SharePoint/OneDrive.

Frontline & Plant Workers (4,500 users)

Who: Manufacturing operators, logistics, shift workers, plant supervisors

Location: Manufacturing plants, distribution centers

Devices: Mobile (iOS/Android), Shared tablets, Kiosks

Primary Use Cases:
Shift updates Quick coordination Shift huddles Safety alerts

Communication Pattern: Brief mobile chats. Channels for shift info. Audio/video messages more than text. Offline capability needed. Low-bandwidth friendly.

Executive Leadership (1,500 users)

Who: C-Suite, SVP, VP, Directors, Strategic decision-makers

Location: Global, mostly remote or traveling

Devices: Desktop + Mobile (travel-heavy)

Primary Use Cases:
Strategic discussions Executive meetings Town halls Board info

Communication Pattern: Selective chat users. Preference for scheduled meetings. Heavy reliance on transcription & meeting notes. Requires compliance/legal hold capability.

External Partners & Guests (2,000+ users)

Who: Contractors, consultants, vendors, partners, temporary staff

Location: External, temporary access

Devices: Personal devices (mostly mobile)

Primary Use Cases:
Project collaboration Specific teams Reviews & meetings Knowledge transfer

Communication Pattern: Limited access (specific teams). Temporary accounts (expires after project). DLP & data protection essential. No chat outside approved teams.

IT & Support Teams (1,000 users)

Who: IT admins, support desk, technical teams, security ops

Location: Global support centers

Devices: Desktop (primary), Mobile (secondary)

Primary Use Cases:
Incident coordination Escalation chats Daily standups Knowledge base

Communication Pattern: Incident channels with bots/alerts. Escalation workflows. Heavy integration with monitoring tools. Always-on presence needed.

Architecture & Design

How Teams is designed for enterprise-scale collaboration

Architecture Philosophy: Single global tenant. Cloud-first. Integrated with Microsoft 365. Multi-geo resilience. Identity-driven access. Audit trail on everything.

Core Architecture Principles

Single Global Tenant

One Teams environment for all 15,000 users. No fragmentation. Unified governance. Seamless cross-location collaboration.

Benefit: Global connectivity, single identity source
Multi-Geo Data Residency

Teams data stored in geo-regions (North America, EU, APAC). GDPR and local compliance. Failover redundancy.

Benefit: Compliance + performance + resilience
Chat vs Channel Design

Chat: 1-on-1, informal, transient. Channels: Team knowledge, persistent, organized.

Pattern: Chat for quick decisions, channels for institutional knowledge
Integration Backbone

Teams + SharePoint (files) + OneDrive (personal) + Exchange (email) + Microsoft Graph (APIs).

Result: One unified platform, not scattered tools

Meetings & Video Collaboration

How we meet at scale across 60+ sites

Meeting Vision: Video-first, inclusive, recorded, transcribed, and searchable. Every meeting is an opportunity to build institutional knowledge. We capture, we share, we learn together.

Teams Meeting Standards

Meeting Types

1:1 Calls

Quick calls, no recording (unless confidential). Ad-hoc or scheduled.

Team Meetings

5-50 people. Standups, planning, reviews. Always record for absent members.

Large Meetings (50-500)

All-hands, town halls, webinars. Host controls. Q&A enabled.

Virtual Events (500+)

Webinar mode. Attendees muted. Producer controls. Always recorded.

Meeting Features

  • Auto-recording (unless opted out)
  • Real-time transcription (English, Spanish, etc.)
  • AI-generated summary & highlights
  • Co-authoring during meetings
  • Screen sharing + whiteboarding
  • Calendar integration & reminders

Network & Bandwidth Considerations

Teams meetings run over IP networks. Global scale requires network resilience.

Bandwidth Requirements
  • 1:1 call: 1.2 Mbps
  • Group call: 2.5 Mbps (per participant)
  • HD video: 4 Mbps minimum
  • Screen share: +2.5 Mbps
Network Resilience
  • Multi-path connectivity at plants
  • Failover to mobile if WiFi drops
  • QoS for Teams traffic
  • Monitoring & alerts
