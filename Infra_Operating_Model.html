Cultural Model Across Infra
Culture is the real operating system.
4 common traits · 3 anti-patterns · 1 infra culture playbook
Common Cultural Traits
Shared across all domains.
  • Psychological safety: engineers can stop a change with a single concern; 0 negative performance reviews tied to raising risk.
  • Blameless postmortems for all P1 incidents; themes tracked and rolled into quarterly improvement backlog.
  • Automation over heroics: manual “hero saves” celebrated less than sustainable fixes; 44% of toil removed in 2 years.
  • Documentation as a first-class asset: documentation quality is part of performance expectations.

Anti-Patterns to Eliminate
What is not tolerated.
  • Ticket ping-pong: MTTR increases when ownership is unclear; now blocked via routing rules and playbooks.
  • Silo pride: KPIs and rewards are aligned to cross-domain outcomes, not local optimizations.
  • “Not my team” mindset: incident bridges are organized by services and impact, not by reporting lines.
The operating model works only if culture supports cross-team empathy, learning, and shared accountability.
