Architecture Principles
Design Guiding Principles
🧩

Disaggregation First

Prefer open, disaggregated architectures (OLT, RAN, routing) to reduce vendor lock-in and enable best-of-breed component selection.

⚡

Automation by Default

Every provisioning, change, and remediation workflow should have an automation-first design. Manual fallback only where exception-handling is required.

🔐

Zero Trust Security

Identity-based access, micro-segmentation, encrypted transport, and continuous verification across all network and platform layers.

📡

Telemetry Native

All network elements must support streaming telemetry (gNMI / gRPC). SNMP-only devices to be phased out or wrapped with telemetry proxies.

☁️

Cloud Integration Ready

Core platforms and data pipelines to support multi-cloud deployment, hyperscaler peering, and cloud-native workload hosting.

📈

Scalable by Design

Architecture must support 3x subscriber growth and 5x traffic growth over 5 years without linear cost or headcount scaling.

Portfolio of services and SLA commitments
▼

Operations Layer
NOC, field, change, and capacity operations
▼
Network Operations Center (NOC) Service Assurance Field Operations & Dispatch Change Operations Capacity Management Vendor Operations Management Event & Incident Command

The execution core of the model. Converges fiber, wireless, and IT operations into a unified NOC model with tiered L1/L2/L3 support and integrated escalation paths.

4
Platform & Tooling Layer
Integrated technology platforms enabling operations
▼
Unified Observability Platform ITSM Platform (ITIL v4) Network Inventory System CMDB (Live, Validated) Automation Platform (Ansible / Terraform) Data Lake & Analytics API Gateway & Integration Bus

Provides the technology substrate for all operations. Platforms are integrated via APIs with bidirectional data flows between ITSM, observability, inventory, and automation engines.

5
Automation Layer
Closed-loop automation and AI-assisted workflows
▼
Service Provisioning Automation Incident Enrichment & Triage Auto-Ticket Creation Root Cause Recommendation Dynamic SLA Adjustment Automated Post-Change Validation Self-Healing Network Actions

Executes automated workflows triggered by observability events, ITSM tickets, or scheduled jobs. Reduces manual effort and accelerates MTTR through pre-approved runbooks.

6
Governance Layer
Decision-making, oversight, and accountability structures
▼
Change Advisory Board (CAB) Service Review Boards Architecture Review Board Vendor Performance Governance Risk & Compliance Review Security Operations Board

Provides structured oversight across all operational and transformation activities. Governance forums meet on defined cadences and produce actionable decisions with ownership.

7
Continuous Improvement Layer
Systematic improvement cycles and performance reviews
▼
KPI Review Cadence Problem Management Reviews Automation Backlog Management Service Improvement Plans (SIPs) Quarterly Roadmap Refresh Lessons Learned Repository

Ensures the operating model evolves continuously. Feeds insights from incident post-mortems, KPI gaps, and automation performance back into the roadmap and process designs.

Interview Questionnaire

Assessment Interview Guide

A structured question framework for executive, technical, and operational stakeholder interviews — designed to surface evidence-based insights for the assessment.

🎯
Executive & Strategic Questions
  • 1What are the top 3 strategic goals for the organization over the next 3 years, and how does operational capability need to change to support them?
  • 2Which services are most critical for revenue growth, and what operational risks currently threaten their delivery?
  • 3What customer experience issues are most visible to leadership, and how are they currently being tracked and reported?
  • 4How are technology investment decisions made, and what is the governance framework for prioritization?
  • 5What does success look like for this transformation in 18 months?
📡
Network Engineering Questions
  • 1What are the top 5 recurring network incidents by frequency and impact over the last 12 months?
  • 2Which parts of the fiber or wireless network have known capacity constraints or are approaching saturation?
  • 3What tools are currently used for monitoring, fault management, and troubleshooting — and are they integrated?
  • 4How is network change management currently governed, and what is the failure or rollback rate?
  • 5What is the current state of network inventory and CMDB accuracy?
⚙️
Operations & Service Management Questions
  • 1How are incidents currently detected, triaged, routed, and resolved — and where are the biggest bottlenecks?
  • 2What percentage of changes fail or require rollback, and what are the most common causes?
  • 3How mature is the CMDB — what percentage of active CIs are accurately documented with current relationships?
  • 4How are SLAs currently tracked, reported, and enforced across service tiers?
  • 5How are problem management and root cause analysis conducted after major incidents?
🤖
Automation & Data Questions
  • 1Which daily or weekly activities are highly repetitive and would be the strongest candidates for automation?
  • 2What structured data is available today that could support AI/ML-driven operations (tickets, telemetry, change records)?
  • 3Are post-change validations automated today, and if not, how are they performed?
  • 4What automation exists today, and what prevented it from being scaled or integrated with ITSM?
  • 5How confident is the team in the quality and completeness of the data needed for AI models?
🤝
Vendor & Governance Questions
  • 1Which vendors support core platforms, and are their SLAs contractually defined and regularly measured?
  • 2How are vendor performance reviews currently conducted, and are they standardized across all vendors?
  • 3What is the current level of vendor lock-in risk for critical network or platform components?
  • 4Are there open API commitments from all strategic vendors — and are they being utilized?
  • 5How are vendor escalations handled, and who has accountability for vendor relationship management?
