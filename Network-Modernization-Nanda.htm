Vision & Operating Principles

Strategic Foundation & Guiding Philosophy

Network Vision Statement

Our North Star

Transform our global network into an intelligent, self-healing, zero-trust fabric that powers business velocity while delivering carrier-grade reliability, predictive operations, and seamless multi-cloud connectivity.


We envision a network that is invisible to users, anticipates issues before they impact business, adapts dynamically to changing demands, and serves as a competitive differentiator through automation, insight-driven optimization, and unwavering security.

Six Operating Principles
1. Resilience by Design
Build redundancy at every layer. Eliminate single points of failure through active-active architectures, diverse path routing, and automated failover mechanisms.
2. Zero Trust Everywhere
Never trust, always verify. Implement identity-based access, micro-segmentation, and encrypted transport end-to-end with continuous authentication.
3. Automation-First
Eliminate manual toil through infrastructure-as-code, CI/CD pipelines, and event-driven workflows. Target 80%+ automation coverage.
4. Observability-Driven
Instrument everything. Collect telemetry, establish baselines, and enable predictive analytics through comprehensive monitoring.
5. Standardization with Governance
Reduce complexity through consistent design patterns, approved vendor stack, and modular architectures with policy-as-code.
6. FinOps & Cost Optimization
Treat network spend as strategic investment. Continuously optimize bandwidth utilization and measure ROI on initiatives.

Current-State Assessment

Three-Phase Discovery & Baseline Program

A

Phase A: Discover & Baseline (Weeks 0-6)

Objective: Establish comprehensive inventory and performance baseline

Key Activities

  • Complete network discovery across all 2,000 branches, 400 OT sites, 20 colo hubs, and 6 DCs
  • Document device inventory, software versions, configurations, and topology maps
  • Collect 4-6 weeks of performance metrics (availability, latency, utilization, errors)
  • Interview 15-20 stakeholders (NetOps, SecOps, App teams, business units)
  • Review incident tickets, change records, and capacity planning data
B

Phase B: Diagnose & Prioritize (Weeks 6-10)

Objective: Identify gaps, risks, and improvement opportunities

Key Activities

  • Conduct maturity assessment across 12 capability domains
  • Identify technical debt, end-of-life equipment, and security vulnerabilities
  • Analyze incident patterns, root causes, and service impact
  • Benchmark against industry standards and peer organizations
  • Calculate total cost of ownership (TCO) for current environment
C

Phase C: Decide Target Outcomes (Weeks 10-12)

Objective: Define future state and secure executive alignment

Key Activities

  • Facilitate executive workshops to prioritize business outcomes
  • Define target maturity levels (2-3 level jump over 24-30 months)
  • Develop high-level business case with ROI projections
  • Socialize vision with key stakeholders and secure sponsorship
  • Establish success metrics and governance framework

Modernization Strategy

Five-Stream Approach for Network Transformation

Strategic Workstreams

Multi-Stream Transformation Approach

Our modernization strategy executes through five parallel workstreams, each with dedicated teams, milestones, and success criteria. This approach ensures comprehensive coverage while maintaining business continuity.

Five Core Streams

  • Infrastructure Modernization: Hardware refresh, SD-WAN deployment, cloud connectivity
  • Automation & Orchestration: IaC adoption, CI/CD pipelines, self-service portals
  • Security Transformation: Zero Trust implementation, SASE adoption, threat intelligence
  • Observability & Analytics: Unified monitoring, AIOps, predictive analytics
  • Organizational Change: Training, process redesign, culture transformation
