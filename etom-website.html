What is eTOM?

The Enhanced Telecom Operations Map — the global business process framework for telecommunications service providers, published as TM Forum standard GB921.

📘 eTOM is the telecom industry's equivalent of ITIL for enterprise IT. It defines what processes a telco needs — not how to implement them.
Definition
The Enhanced Telecom Operations Map
A comprehensive, hierarchical map of all business processes a telecommunications service provider must manage — from customer-facing service delivery to back-office network and supplier operations.

📋 Published By

TM Forum (tmforum.org) as standard GB921. Current version is eTOM Release 21, aligned with Frameworx 21.5.

🌍 Industry Adoption

Used by 850+ telecom operators across 180+ countries. The de facto process standard for global telcos.

🎯 Purpose

Defines what business processes a telco needs — not how to implement them. Provides a common language for process design and improvement.

🔗 Relation to ITIL

eTOM covers telecom-specific operations — network, service, billing, and customer. ITIL covers IT service management. They are complementary, not competing.

📦 Frameworx Suite

eTOM is part of TM Forum Frameworx — alongside SID (data model), TAM (application map), and MPLS (integration maturity).

The Power of Process Hierarchy

Level 3 — Process Definitions

The primary unit of process definition in eTOM — 180+ processes with clear objectives, inputs, outputs, and inter-process dependencies.

Key L3 Processes for Telecom Operations
Service Problem Management (1.2.2)
The highest-priority L3 process cluster for NOC operations — governs how service faults are detected, managed, and resolved.
Assurance · Service Problem Management · 1.2.2
1.2.2.1
Create Service Problem Report
Creates a structured problem record when a service fault is detected — from network alarm, customer report, or proactive monitoring event.
1.2.2.2
Diagnose Service Problem
Analyses the problem record to identify root cause — correlating alarms, telemetry, and CMDB data to isolate the fault domain.
1.2.2.3
Correct & Resolve Service Problem
Executes remediation — configuration changes, traffic rerouting, or field dispatch — to restore the affected service.
1.2.2.4
Track & Manage Service Problem
Maintains the lifecycle of the problem record through all stages, ensuring SLA timers are tracked and escalations triggered when required.
1.2.2.5
Report Service Problem
Provides structured reporting on service problems — volumes, trends, root cause categories, repeat incidents, and SLA performance.
1.2.2.6
Close Service Problem Report
Confirms restoration, validates with monitoring system, and closes the record with resolution details and knowledge base update.
Order Handling & Service Activation (1.1.1 / 1.1.4)
L3 processes governing the order-to-activate pipeline for broadband and wireless services.
Fulfillment · Order Handling (1.1.1) + Service Config & Activation (1.1.4)
1.1.1.1
Determine Customer Order Feasibility
Validates whether the requested service can be delivered at the customer location given available infrastructure and capacity.
1.1.1.2
Track & Manage Customer Order
Tracks the order through all fulfillment stages, managing handoffs between OSS, provisioning, and field systems.
1.1.1.3
Complete Customer Order
Confirms all order milestones are met, triggers service acceptance, and closes the order record.
1.1.4.1
Design Service
Translates the ordered service into a specific network configuration — IP addressing, VLAN, PON port assignment, etc.
1.1.4.2
Implement Service
Pushes configuration to OSS/BSS and downstream network elements — OLT, CMTS, RAN controller, or router.
1.1.4.3
Test Service End-to-End
Executes automated and manual tests to confirm service meets contracted performance levels before customer notification.
1.1.4.4
Activate Service
Marks the service as live in inventory and BSS systems, triggers customer notification, and starts SLA and billing clocks.
Resource Trouble & Performance (1.5.2 / 1.5.3)
Resource Management · Resource Trouble (1.5.2) + Resource Performance (1.5.3)
1.5.2.1
Survey & Analyse Resource Trouble
Detects alarms from network elements, correlates events, and identifies the affected resource scope.
1.5.2.2

Pinpoints the specific network element, port, or circuit that is the source of the fault — fault isolation.
1.5.2.3

1.5.2.4

1.5.3.2

1.5.3.3

Recommendations

Key Recommendations for eTOM Adoption

Eight priority actions to embed eTOM into the future operating model and maximise the value of the transformation program.

1

Adopt eTOM as the Process Standard

Define eTOM L1–L4 as the mandatory process taxonomy for all telecom operations. All ITSM workflows, SOPs, and runbooks must reference eTOM process IDs.

2

Map Current Processes to eTOM First

Before redesigning any process, map what exists today to the eTOM hierarchy. This reveals gaps and prevents reinventing processes that eTOM already defines.

3

Prioritise Assurance L3 Processes

Service Problem Management (1.2.2) and Resource Trouble Management (1.5.2) deliver the highest MTTR and MTTD improvements. Design these first.

4

Target L4 Automation in Fulfillment

The highest ROI L4 automation is in Service Configuration and Activation (1.1.4) — auto-config generation and auto-CMDB update can reduce provisioning time by 80%.

5

Integrate eTOM IDs into ITSM

Tag every ITSM ticket, workflow, and SLA with its corresponding eTOM L3 process ID. This enables process-level performance reporting and benchmarking.

6

Use eTOM for CMDB Relationship Design

The Resource Data Management process (1.5.4) defines what data the CMDB must hold. Use it as the specification for the CMDB remediation sprint in Phase 2.

7

Align Vendor Contracts to eTOM SLAs

Reference eTOM process areas in vendor SLA contracts — particularly Supplier Problem Reporting (1.6.3) — to create standardised accountability and measurement.

8

Score and Report eTOM Maturity Quarterly

Establish a quarterly eTOM maturity review at L2 and L3 level, reported to the Service Operations Board and Steering Committee as part of the KPI dashboard.

Reference

eTOM Quick Reference Glossary

Key terms and concepts for working with the eTOM framework and TM Forum Frameworx standards.

eTOM
Enhanced Telecom Operations Map. TM Forum's business process framework (GB921) defining all telecom business processes in a hierarchical structure.
TM Forum
The global industry association for digital transformation in communications and media. Publisher of eTOM, SID, and TAM standards.
Frameworx
TM Forum's integrated suite of standards — eTOM (processes), SID (data model), TAM (application architecture), and MPLS (integration maturity).
GB921
The TM Forum document reference number for the eTOM specification. "GB" stands for Guideline Book, the TM Forum's published standard series.
L1 Area
The highest-level grouping in eTOM. Examples: Fulfillment, Assurance, Billing. Defines strategic operational domains within the Operations zone.
L2 Grouping
Functional cluster within an L1 area. Examples: Order Handling, Service Problem Management. Typically aligns with team or department boundaries.
L3 Process
The primary unit of process definition. Has a specific objective, inputs, outputs, and inter-process dependencies. 180+ processes in the full eTOM.
L4 Activity
The most granular level — individual steps, decision points, and handoffs within an L3 process. Where automation runbooks and SOPs are defined.
OSS
Operations Support System. IT systems supporting network management, provisioning, and assurance. Maps to eTOM Resource Management processes.
BSS
Business Support System. IT systems supporting billing, ordering, and customer management. Maps to eTOM Fulfillment and Billing processes.
SID
Shared Information and Data Model. TM Forum's companion standard to eTOM defining the data model for all telecom business entities and relationships.
TAM
Telecom Application Map. TM Forum standard mapping applications to eTOM process areas — equivalent to the tool mapping in this assessment.
