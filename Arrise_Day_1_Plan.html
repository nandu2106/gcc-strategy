ARRISE Studio IT Leadership Plan

Day 1, First 2 Weeks, and First 30 Days plan for establishing trust, understanding the business, stabilizing operations, and building a clear execution roadmap with CTO Ankit.

Role: Head of Studio IT Focus: Stability + Scale + Ownership Audience: CTO / Leadership Prepared by: Nanda Karri

Executive Intent

This plan is designed to show that I am entering ARRISE with a clear leadership mindset: listen first, understand the business, stabilize operations, build trusted relationships, and then drive structured improvements.

Leadership message: My first 30 days will be focused on understanding business-critical studio operations, building stakeholder trust, identifying operational risks, and creating a practical roadmap that improves reliability, ownership, and execution maturity.

Day 1 Theme

Listen, align, and understand expectations.

  • Meet CTO and immediate stakeholders
  • Understand top priorities
  • Clarify success expectations
  • Set communication rhythm

First 2 Weeks Theme

Discover current state and operational reality.

  • Understand people, process, tools, and vendors
  • Review incidents and recurring problems
  • Map business-critical services
  • Identify quick wins and risk areas

First 30 Days Theme

Create operating model and improvement roadmap.

  • Define priorities
  • Improve visibility and governance
  • Build 30/60/90 day execution view
  • Present findings to CTO

Guiding Principles

Principle Meaning How I Will Apply It
Business First Technology should support studio uptime, business continuity, and productivity. Start with business-critical services, not just IT tools.
Stability Before Change Avoid unnecessary disruption in the first few weeks. Observe, understand, and validate before changing processes.
Visibility Creates Control Strong dashboards, incident data, asset data, and ownership clarity reduce surprises. Review monitoring, ticketing, escalation, and reporting maturity.
People + Process + Technology Most operational problems are not only technical; they are also ownership and process issues. Assess team structure, RACI, escalation paths, vendor dependencies, and tooling.
Earn Trust Early Senior leaders must create confidence with calm execution. Communicate clearly, listen deeply, and deliver small visible wins.

Day 1 Plan

The first day should be calm, structured, and relationship-focused. The goal is to understand expectations, show maturity, and create early confidence with CTO Ankit and key stakeholders.

1. Opening Positioning

Use this as the tone for the first conversation with CTO Ankit.

“Ankit, I am excited to be here. My first priority is to understand the business, the studio operating model, the critical technology dependencies, and your expectations from this function. I want to listen first, understand the current state, and then build a practical roadmap that improves stability, visibility, and execution.”

2. Day 1 Outcomes

  • Understand CTO’s top 3 expectations.
  • Understand immediate business priorities and pressure points.
  • Identify critical stakeholders to meet in the first 2 weeks.
  • Understand studio IT scope, team structure, and current operating rhythm.
  • Agree on communication cadence with CTO.

Day 1 Meeting Checklist

Meeting / Activity Purpose Questions to Ask Output
CTO 1:1 Understand expectations, priorities, and success definition. What are the top 3 outcomes expected from me in the next 90 days? Leadership expectation notes.
Manager / Buddy / HR Understand onboarding, policies, systems, and access process. What are the key systems and processes I need access to? Access and onboarding checklist.
Immediate Team Introduction Build trust and reduce anxiety. What is working well today? What needs leadership attention? Initial team sentiment.
Operations Overview Understand service coverage and daily operational rhythm. What are the most critical systems, tools, and escalation paths? Initial current-state map.

Day 1 Do's

  • Listen more than you speak.
  • Ask business-oriented questions.
  • Take notes seriously.
  • Show calmness and ownership.
  • Respect existing team efforts.
  • Ask where leadership support is required.

Day 1 Avoid

  • Do not criticize existing systems immediately.
  • Do not promise transformation without understanding reality.
  • Do not go too deep into technical tools unless asked.
  • Do not sound like only an operations manager or only a network engineer.
  • Do not push AI, automation, or modernization before understanding pain points.

End of Day 1 Message to CTO

“Ankit, thank you for the time today. I have captured the key priorities and will spend the next two weeks understanding the current operating model, critical services, team structure, incident patterns, vendor dependencies, and stakeholder expectations. I will then come back with a clear current-state summary, quick wins, risks, and a practical 30/60/90 day roadmap.”

First 2 Weeks Plan

The first two weeks should be used for structured discovery. The goal is to understand the business, people, process, technology, risk areas, and operational maturity before making major changes.

Week 1: Relationship + Discovery

  • Meet direct team members individually.
  • Meet key business stakeholders and studio operations leaders.
  • Understand current studio IT scope and responsibilities.
  • Review org structure, shift coverage, and support model.
  • Review critical services and business-impacting dependencies.
  • Understand current incident management and escalation process.
  • Identify immediate risks or open escalations.

Week 2: Operational Deep Dive

  • Review top recurring incidents from the last 3–6 months.
  • Review service desk data, SLA performance, and ticket aging.
  • Review monitoring tools and alerting quality.
  • Understand asset lifecycle, endpoint readiness, and access management.
  • Review network, connectivity, AV, studio floor, and critical infrastructure touchpoints.
  • Map vendor dependencies and contract pain points.
  • Identify quick wins, medium-term improvements, and strategic gaps.

Discovery Framework

Area What to Understand Evidence to Collect Expected Output
Business Operations How studio operations depend on IT services. Critical business process list, peak hours, downtime impact. Business dependency map.
People Team capability, ownership, shifts, morale, skill gaps. Org chart, roles, responsibilities, coverage model. People and capability assessment.
Process Incident, change, problem, request, and escalation processes. ITSM reports, SLA data, escalation history. Process maturity view.
Technology Network, endpoints, AV, identity, monitoring, collaboration, studio-specific tools. Architecture diagrams, inventory, monitoring dashboards. Technology current-state map.
Vendors Third-party support, SLAs, contracts, recurring delays. Vendor list, contract terms, escalation logs. Vendor dependency and risk view.
Risk Single points of failure, weak controls, unclear ownership, recurring outages. Incident RCA, risk register, audit findings. Initial risk register.

First 2 Weeks Deliverables

  • Stakeholder map: CTO, business leaders, studio operations, HR, finance, procurement, security, vendors, global IT teams.
  • Current-state summary: People, process, technology, tools, and vendor model.
  • Critical service map: Services that directly impact studio uptime and business continuity.
  • Incident pattern review: Top recurring issues and high-severity incidents.
  • Quick wins list: Low-risk improvements that can be delivered quickly.
  • Initial risk register: Key operational, technical, and ownership risks.
  • Leadership update: Short summary to CTO on findings and proposed next steps.

First 30 Days Plan

By the end of 30 days, the goal is to move from discovery to structured execution. This does not mean changing everything immediately. It means creating clarity, priorities, governance, and a practical roadmap.

30-Day Leadership Outcomes

  • Clear understanding of studio IT operating model.
  • Documented stakeholder expectations.
  • Initial operating rhythm with team and leadership.
  • Top risks and recurring issues identified.
  • Quick wins executed or in progress.
  • 30/60/90 day roadmap prepared.
  • Early trust built with CTO, team, and business stakeholders.

30-Day Focus Areas

  • Operational Stability: Incident reduction, escalation clarity, monitoring review.
  • Service Visibility: Dashboards, SLA reports, business-impact view.
  • Ownership Clarity: RACI, team roles, vendor accountability.
  • Risk Reduction: Single points of failure, repeat incidents, weak controls.
  • Stakeholder Confidence: Clear communication and predictable updates.

30-Day Roadmap Structure

Phase Timeline Focus Expected Output
Stabilize Days 1–10 Understand critical services, open risks, team structure, and urgent issues. Current-state notes and priority list.
Diagnose Days 11–20 Analyze incidents, tools, escalation, service ownership, and vendor gaps. Operational maturity assessment.
Structure Days 21–30 Create governance rhythm, risk register, quick wins, and execution roadmap. 30/60/90 day plan for CTO review.

Proposed 30/60/90 Day View

Timeline Goal Key Actions Success Indicator
First 30 Days Understand and stabilize. Discovery, stakeholder mapping, current-state review, incident analysis, risk register, quick wins. CTO has clear visibility of current state and priorities.
Days 31–60 Improve control and predictability. Strengthen governance, dashboards, SLA reviews, RACI, vendor reviews, incident/problem management. Better operational visibility and reduced ambiguity.
Days 61–90 Scale and modernize. Drive automation, monitoring improvements, standardization, service maturity, documentation, and strategic initiatives. Structured transformation roadmap with measurable outcomes.

30-Day CTO Review Format

  1. What I understood about the business and studio operating model.
  2. What is working well today.
  3. Top operational risks and recurring pain points.
  4. Quick wins completed or initiated.
  5. Areas needing leadership attention or investment.
  6. Proposed 60-day execution priorities.
  7. Support needed from CTO or leadership.

CTO Discussion Guide

These questions are designed to make the conversation strategic, mature, and business-aligned. The goal is to understand CTO Ankit’s expectations and align your plan to his priorities.

Questions to Ask CTO Ankit

  • What are the top 3 outcomes you expect from me in the first 90 days?
  • Where do you currently see the biggest operational pain?
  • What does success look like for this role after 6 months?
  • Which business stakeholders should I prioritize meeting first?
  • Are there any ongoing escalations or sensitive areas I should understand immediately?
  • What level of reporting or communication rhythm would you prefer from me?
  • Where do you want me to challenge the current way of working?
  • What should I preserve, and what should I improve?

What You Should Communicate

  • I will listen first before changing anything.
  • I will focus on business-critical services and operational stability.
  • I will build visibility around incidents, risks, vendors, and ownership.
  • I will create a practical roadmap, not a theoretical transformation plan.
  • I will keep leadership informed with concise, structured updates.
  • I will work with the existing team respectfully and build confidence.

Suggested CTO Conversation Script

“Ankit, my approach for the first 30 days is simple. I want to understand the business context, studio criticality, current operating model, team structure, incident patterns, vendor dependencies, and risk areas. I do not want to make assumptions too early. Once I have enough visibility, I will come back with a current-state view, quick wins, key risks, and a 30/60/90 day execution roadmap.”
“My goal is to reduce operational surprises, strengthen ownership, improve visibility, and make sure Studio IT becomes a trusted, predictable, and business-aligned function.”

Risks, Controls, and Early Focus Areas

This section helps capture potential issues that may exist in any business-critical studio IT environment. These should be validated through discovery, not assumed.

Initial Risk Register Template

Risk Area Possible Risk Impact First 30-Day Action
Service Availability Critical systems may have weak redundancy or unclear ownership. Studio downtime, business interruption, customer impact. Map critical services, support owners, and escalation paths.
Incident Management Recurring issues may not have strong RCA or problem management. Repeat incidents and leadership frustration. Review top incidents and create recurring issue tracker.
Monitoring Alerts may be noisy, missing, or not business-impact aligned. Delayed detection and reactive operations. Review monitoring coverage and alert quality.
Vendor Dependency External vendors may delay resolution or lack accountability. SLA misses and operational delays. Create vendor dependency matrix and escalation path.
Access & Identity Access provisioning or privileged access may lack strong governance. Security and compliance exposure. Review access process, approval flow, and audit controls.
Asset Lifecycle Weak endpoint, peripheral, or studio equipment tracking. Support delays, cost leakage, poor user experience. Review inventory accuracy and lifecycle process.
Change Management Changes may not be risk-assessed properly for studio operations. Unplanned downtime or business disruption. Review change process and blackout windows.

Potential Quick Wins

  • Create daily or weekly operational health dashboard.
  • Define top 10 critical services and owners.
  • Review high-aging tickets and unblock them.
  • Create simple escalation matrix.
  • Document recurring incidents and assign owners.
  • Improve shift handover quality.
  • Introduce weekly vendor review for open critical items.
  • Improve communication templates for outages and incidents.

Early Metrics to Review

  • Number of P1/P2 incidents.
  • Mean Time to Detect.
  • Mean Time to Restore.
  • Repeat incident count.
  • SLA breach rate.
  • Ticket backlog and aging.
  • Change success rate.
  • Vendor SLA performance.
  • Endpoint readiness and asset accuracy.
  • User satisfaction or business stakeholder feedback.
